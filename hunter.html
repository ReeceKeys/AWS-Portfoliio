Manual DVT Testing

At Hunter Industries, I gained hands-on experience performing manual DVT (Design Verification Test) procedures on various in-house irrigation controllers and PCBs. This process involved validating hardware functions, stress-testing electrical interfaces, and confirming firmware interactions.

My responsibilities included interpreting electrical schematics, probing board-level signals with oscilloscopes and logic analyzers, and meticulously recording test outcomes. This testing phase was critical to uncovering edge-case failures and ensuring the hardware conformed to design specifications before mass production.

Low Level Communications

In parallel with manual testing, I modernized a legacy Visual Basic logging utility into a robust C++ application. This migration improved performance, modularity, and maintainability — allowing the QA team to scale up testing and streamline data collection.

In this project, I engineered a custom RS485-based communication layer between the C++ application and Hunter’s embedded logging boards. This replaced expensive third-party tools and enabled precise real-time interaction with hardware devices. The resulting automation framework reduced test cycle time, enabled repeatable validation scenarios, and improved visibility into device behavior.
